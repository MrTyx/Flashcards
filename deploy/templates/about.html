What is this?

This is a project for a course as part of my Masters of Computer Science. I needed to design a use case where users interact with data on Google Cloud using a web interface, and then to perform analytics on that data using an algorithm of my choice.


How are review times calculated?

On the backend, review times are calculated using a Spaced Repetition Algorithm. A card must first be studied. It is then due for review in twelve hours. As a flashcard is reviewed, the user scores themself. That score applies a multiplier to the due time. If a user scores Normal, the time between reviews will grow at a steady rate.

The minimum review time is two hours. As you reach a stage where you can honestly score yourself Normal or Easy, the time will increase.

ScoreMultiplier
Forgot0.1
Hard0.75
Normal1.25
Easy2.5

Example: Alice reviews a flashcard. The due time is 12 hours (43200 seconds). When she reviews the flashcard she scores it as Hard. The flashcard is now due in 9 hours (43200 x 0.75 = 32400 seconds). When she reviews the flashcard for a second time, she scores it as Normal. The flashcard is now due in 11.25 hours (32400 x 1.25 = 40500 seconds).


How many cards should I study/review?

Ignore any advice if you get better results at a different pace. That said, this is my recommendation;

Twice daily, start every session by reviewing up to fifty cards. If you reviewed less than fifty, then always study. But if you reviewed fifty, then gauge if you feel up to some study. If you are studying, do one session of ten new cards.

There is a threshold where you get diminishing returns on any study or review. Most spaced repetition algorithms put this at 100 flashcards. I would recommend starting at 50 flashcards per session, two sessions per day.
